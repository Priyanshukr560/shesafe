About Us

Empowering women with safety, confidence, and peace of mind. Our app is designed to prioritize women’s security in every situation, whether at home, work, or on the move. With features like real-time location sharing, emergency SOS alerts, and community support networks, we provide tools that keep women connected and safeguarded. Our mission is to create a world where technology bridges the gap between vulnerability and empowerment. Whether it’s a late-night commute, an unfamiliar neighborhood, or seeking trusted contacts, we’ve got you covered. Together, let’s make safety simple, accessible, and proactive.

    • Social Data

    Education:In India, 86.5% of girls complete lower secondary school, which is higher than the average for South Asia and other lower-middle-income countries. However, adult literacy among women in India is 70%, which is lower than the global average.

    Labor Force Participation: Globally, women’s labor force participation is often lower than men’s. In India, only 31.2% of women participate in the labor force compared to 76.8% of men.

    Violence Against Women: In India, 18.4% of women aged 15–49 reported experiencing physical or sexual violence by a partner in the past year (as of 2018)

    Political Representation: Women hold only 14.7% of seats in India’s parliament, reflecting the global challenge of achieving gender parity in political representation.

    Global Perspective: The UN Declaration on the Elimination of Violence Against Women highlights that such crimes stem from historically imbalanced power dynamics between genders

    Solutions and Initiatives

    Government Initiatives in India:

    The "Nirbhaya" Fund was established to support projects aimed at enhancing women’s safety. The Women Safety Division under the Ministry of Home Affairs focuses on strategic planning and execution of safety measures.

    Role of Technology:

    Safety apps like SOS alerts, GPS tracking, and community-driven platforms are empowering women to feel more secure
    smart devices and wearable tech are also being developed to provide real-time assistance.

    Challenges:

    Despite legal frameworks, societal attitudes and delayed justice remain significant barriers. Public spaces often lack adequate safety measures, leading to widespread fear among women.

    Global Perspective:

    Countries like Denmark and Switzerland are considered safer for women due to robust laws and societal norms. India ranks 128th out of 177 countries in the Women Peace and Security Index, reflecting the need for improvement
